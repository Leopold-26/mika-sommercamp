# Onboarding Question Bank

Use these questions selectively. Read current docs and uploads first, then ask only what is missing.

## Current Standpoint

1. What already exists?
2. Where are the relevant documents, links, screenshots, mockups, or code?
3. What work should Mika not ask you to repeat?
4. What has already been decided?
5. What is still fuzzy?
6. What have you already tested with users?
7. What feedback or data exists?

## Founder

1. What is your background?
2. Are you technical, semi-technical, or non-technical?
3. What are you unusually good at?
4. Where do you usually get stuck?
5. What part of building this product feels outside your skillset?
6. What do you want Mika/Codex to handle aggressively?
7. How much pushback do you want from Mika?

## Product

1. What do you want to build?
2. Who is the first narrow user?
3. What painful moment does this solve?
4. What does the user currently do instead?
5. What is the smallest version that could be useful?
6. What must happen in the first session?
7. What should not be built before launch?

## Motivation

1. Why this idea?
2. What would make the 10 weeks a success?
3. What would make the 10 weeks a failure?
4. What do you personally want to learn?

## Distribution

1. Where can the first 100 users come from?
2. Which communities or channels do you understand?
3. Do you have an audience, network, or partner access?
4. What could users share?
5. What is the story angle for Gründerszene?
6. What metric should move in the first 7 days after launch?

## Constraints

1. How much time can you work each week?
2. What budget or tools are available?
3. Are there data, privacy, or legal risks?
4. What integrations or accounts already exist?
5. What must be avoided?

## Working Style

1. Do you want Mika to be more coach, PM, developer, or accountability partner?
2. Should Mika explain technical topics in detail or keep them short?
3. How should Mika react when you drift?
4. What usually helps you make decisions?
