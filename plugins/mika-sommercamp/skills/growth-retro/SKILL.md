# Growth Retro

Use this skill weekly or after a launch/distribution experiment.

Read the nearest available `references/gstack-quality-bar.md` for the evidence standard and stop/proceed gates.

## Specialist Role

Act like a growth partner after the work is exposed to reality. Your job is to separate motion from learning and force the next decision: continue, narrow, pivot channel, pivot user, pivot product, or kill.

## Inputs

Read:

- `docs/sommercamp/ten-week-plan.md`
- `docs/sommercamp/distribution-plan.md`
- `docs/sommercamp/build-log.md`
- `docs/sommercamp/editorial-log.md`
- any metrics or user feedback the founder provides

## 95% Understanding Gate

Before deciding, understand:

- what shipped or changed,
- who saw it,
- how many people were reached,
- activation or conversion result,
- retention or repeat signal if available,
- qualitative user feedback,
- what the founder expected,
- what surprised them,
- what was avoided.

If metrics are missing, ask for the smallest available proxy: number of messages sent, replies, visits, signups, demos, completed flows, screenshots, or direct quotes.

## Questions

Ask:

1. What shipped?
2. What did users actually do?
3. What metric moved?
4. What surprised us?
5. What did not work?
6. What did the founder avoid?
7. What should be cut?
8. Decision: continue, narrow, pivot channel, pivot user, pivot product, or kill?

## Retro Scorecard

Rate 0-10:

- **Shipping discipline**
- **User contact**
- **Activation signal**
- **Retention/repeat signal**
- **Distribution learning**
- **Founder focus**
- **Next-decision clarity**

If user contact is below 5, the next week must prioritize distribution or interviews unless there is a launch-blocking bug.

## Decision Rules

- Continue: users completed the core flow and the next bottleneck is clear.
- Narrow: some users care, but the segment or use case is too broad.
- Pivot channel: product signal exists, acquisition path failed.
- Pivot user: pain exists elsewhere, current user is weak.
- Pivot product: user exists, but proposed solution misses the pain.
- Kill: no evidence of urgency after real attempts.

Do not recommend another feature sprint unless it directly addresses an observed user or activation bottleneck.

## Document Updates

Append to `docs/sommercamp/weekly-retros.md`.

If the story is useful externally, route to `$editorial-brief`.

## Output

End with:

- what was shipped,
- what users actually did,
- strongest learning,
- biggest uncomfortable truth,
- retro scorecard,
- decision,
- decision state: `Continue`, `Narrow`, `Pivot channel`, `Pivot user`, `Pivot product`, or `Kill`,
- next weekly goal,
- updated file,
- next route: `$distribution-review`, `$product-wedge-review`, `$build-sprint`, `$website-builder`, `$launch-readiness`, or `$editorial-brief`.
